**Responsible Social Media Use — Do's and Don'ts for College Students**

**DO'S**

1. Think before you post — ask yourself if you'd be comfortable with a future employer seeing it.
2. Use privacy settings — review and tighten who can see your profile and posts.
3. Verify before sharing — check that news or claims are from credible sources before reposting.
4. Engage respectfully — disagree with ideas, not people, and keep discussions constructive.
5. Set screen-time limits — protect study time and sleep by scheduling intentional breaks from apps.
6. Report harmful content — use platform tools to flag harassment, misinformation, or abuse.

**DON'TS**

1. Don't overshare personal information — avoid posting your address, phone number, or daily schedule publicly.
2. Don't cyberbully or troll — mocking, threatening, or harassing others online has real consequences.
3. Don't post under pressure or anger — emotional posts are hard to take back; draft it, sleep on it, then decide.
4. Don't share others' content without permission — reposting photos or private messages can violate trust and copyright.
5. Don't compare your life to feeds — social media shows highlights; unfollow accounts that hurt your self-esteem.
6. Don't use the same password everywhere — weak or reused passwords make all your accounts vulnerable at once.
